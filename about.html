Who am I?

I'm a graduate student in industrial automation at HoGent.
Planning on studying computer science next year. Currently I am working on my thesis. My thesis is focused around improving the practical side of our education and linking the subjects together and to the real world.
You can read more about my thesis here.

I am passionate about learning and teaching, in fact it is my dream to become a teacher one day.
I am a big supporter of (open) education and the open-source movement. I will document all the personal projects that I start (and finish) here. I will document them, whether they will be successful or not. They might form an inspiration or the basis for someone else. Perhaps I will find the solution at a later time, or someone will suggest it to me. We can all learn from each other and we all have something that we can teach others. That is the mindset behind this website.

I am enormously interested in computer and technology, especially when it is vintage (or antique). A lot of projects will be focused around that subject. I also like to work with purely digital electronics and discrete electronics. One of the projects I will start first is going to be a DIY computer using logic chips.

I also like to work with more modern platforms. My favourite being Raspberry Pi and Python. I have experience with other platforms as well, and will use those in projects too. All in all, there should be a nice variety of projects to come (or already here).

If you want to help out, have an idea, or work together; the easiest way to get in touch is to fill out the contact form on the contact page. Alternatively you can use any of the social media links found below.
